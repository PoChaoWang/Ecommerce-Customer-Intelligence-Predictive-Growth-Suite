# Executive Overview
Commentary

This Executive Overview provides a high-level snapshot of customer value, segment structure, historical revenue contribution, and sentiment-related risk. The dashboard covers 8,635 customers, generating 10.36M in total historical revenue, with an average revenue per customer of 1,200. There are also 1,324 customers flagged with sentiment risk, indicating a meaningful customer experience issue that should be monitored.

The largest customer group is General Potential Customer, representing 61.6% of the customer base. This segment also contributes the largest share of historical revenue at 39.2%, mainly because of its large customer volume. However, its average monetary value is much lower than the high-value segments, which suggests that this group should be managed with scalable, low-cost engagement strategies rather than high-touch retention efforts.

VVIP Loyal High-Value Customer has the highest average monetary value, followed closely by Dormant High-Value Customer. This indicates that the business has a strong high-value customer base, but part of that value is not currently active. Dormant High-Value Customers should therefore be a key reactivation target, as they have already demonstrated strong historical purchasing value.

At-Risk Repeat Customer and Dormant High-Value Customer show the highest sentiment risk share among the segments. This is important because both groups have existing purchase history and business value. Improving their customer experience, understanding their negative feedback, and designing targeted retention actions could help reduce churn risk and recover future revenue potential.

Overall, the main business takeaway is that customer volume and customer value are not evenly distributed. General Potential Customers drive total revenue through scale, while VVIP and Dormant High-Value Customers represent stronger individual value. The next step should be to connect these segment insights with action prioritization, especially for dormant high-value users and repeat customers with sentiment risk.
